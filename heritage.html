Pick Your Trail

Taj Mahal

An ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in Agra.

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, is an iconic symbol of love. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Learn more (Wikipedia) Official Website

Red Fort

A historic fort in the city of Delhi in India, which served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors.

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi, India. It was constructed by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century.

Learn more (Wikipedia) Official Website

Ellora Caves

A complex of rock-cut caves in Maharashtra, featuring Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments.

The Ellora Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a group of rock-cut caves in Maharashtra, India. They represent a blend of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments dating back to the 6th and 9th centuries.

Learn more (Wikipedia) Official Website

Qutub Minar

A 73-meter tall victory tower with inscriptions of verses from the Qur'an, located in Delhi.

Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, India. It is a magnificent 73-meter tall tower built in the early 13th century. The tower features intricate carvings and inscriptions.

Learn more (Wikipedia) Official Website

Khajuraho Group of Monuments

A group of Hindu temples and Jain temples in Madhya Pradesh, known for their nagara-style architectural symbolism and their erotic sculptures.

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Madhya Pradesh, India. These temples are famous for their intricate and erotic sculptures, as well as their impressive architecture.

Learn more (Wikipedia) Official Website

Elephanta Caves

A network of sculpted caves located on Elephanta Island in Mumbai Harbor, known for their rock-cut architecture and Hindu sculptures.

The Elephanta Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a collection of rock-cut temples on Elephanta Island near Mumbai, India. The caves feature impressive Hindu sculptures and architecture.

Learn more (Wikipedia) Official Website
