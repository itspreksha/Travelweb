Kaziranga National Park

Home to one-horned rhinoceros

Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Assam, India. It is famous for its population of one-horned rhinoceros, among other wildlife species.

The park also boasts a significant population of tigers, elephants, and various bird species.

Valley of Flowers

Colorful meadows and diverse flora

The Valley of Flowers National Park, located in Uttarakhand, India, is renowned for its stunning meadows adorned with vibrant flowers.

Visitors can witness a variety of flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species such as the snow leopard and Asiatic black bear.

Learn more (Wikipedia) Official Website

Sundarbans National Park

World's largest mangrove forest

The Sundarbans National Park, located in West Bengal, India, is the largest mangrove forest in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

It is home to the Bengal tiger, saltwater crocodile, and numerous bird species.
